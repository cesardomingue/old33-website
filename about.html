Our Story

Built on Grit, Driven by Flavor

At the Crossroads of History & Flavor

In October 2020, at a time when the world felt uncertain, entrepreneur Rey Gotay saw an opportunity in the heart of Elkton. With a vision to transform the historic 159 W Rockingham Street — a staple of the downtown landscape — he launched Old 33 Beer & Burger Grill.

Long before we were a town, this corner was known as Conrad's Store — a simple crossroads where neighbors met and travelers found rest. We took our name from U.S. Route 33 (Spotswood Trail), the legendary path that guided the first explorers over the Blue Ridge Mountains and into the Shenandoah Valley for generations.

Today, that same tradition of hospitality lives on. Whether you're a local coming off a long shift, a hiker descending from the Skyline Drive, or a family stopping in from Massanutten Resort — you're greeted like a regular. Every time.

The Founder

Rey Gotay

Rey Gotay is a career entrepreneur who has been building businesses since the age of 19. When the pandemic shut down the world in 2020, Rey moved in the opposite direction — betting on Elkton, betting on community, and betting on great food.

He took over the historic 159 W Rockingham Street — formerly home to Ciro's Italian Eatery — and completely transformed it. Out went the old-school pizza shop vibe. In came a modern-industrial burger grill that matches the energy of the Elkton Historic District. In the early days, Rey was there every morning at dawn, building something the town could be proud of.

"We don't just flip burgers — we build community. One pint and one patty at a time."

— Rey Gotay, Founder & Owner

Elkton, Virginia

Rooted in History

Our building stands in the heart of the Elkton Historic District — the same corner that shaped a region.

01
Conrad's Store

Long before Elkton was Elkton, this crossroads was known as Conrad's Store — a gathering place for settlers, traders, and travelers heading west over the Blue Ridge.

02
The Railroad Era

In 1881, the Shenandoah Valley Railroad arrived, transforming this quiet crossroads into a bustling commercial hub. Our building reflects that industrial heritage in every brick.

03
Route 33 — Spotswood Trail

We're named after U.S. Route 33, the historic Spotswood Trail that has been the lifeblood of this valley for centuries — connecting Elkton to the world beyond the mountains.

04
The Gateway Location

Positioned at the intersection of Routes 33 and 340 — the literal main and main of Elkton — we are the first stop for hikers off Skyline Drive and the last stop heading into the mountains.
